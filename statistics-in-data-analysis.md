# Statistics in Data Analysis

## Standard Deviation

- **Mean** gives the center of numbers.

- **Standard deviation** measures how far numbers usually sit from that center.

### Example dataset

10, 10, 10, 10, 10

Mean = 10

Every value sits exactly on the mean.

Spread = almost zero.

Standard deviation ≈ 0.

### Another dataset:

2, 5, 10, 15, 18

Mean = 10

Numbers are scattered around 10.

Spread is larger.

Standard deviation is larger.

Meaning

Standard deviation answers:

On average, how far values move away from the mean.

## Why Standard Deviation Is Used

To understand consistency vs variability.

### Example

Two classes.

#### Class A scores

70, 71, 70, 72, 71

- Mean = 70.8
- Std Dev = very small.

Students perform consistently.

#### Class B scores

30, 50, 70, 90, 100

Mean = also around 68–70.

But results vary heavily.

Std Dev = very large.

Mean tells center.

Standard deviation tells stability.

## What “Spread” Means

Spread = how scattered numbers are.

### Three levels

#### Low spread

48, 49, 50, 51, 52

#### Medium spread

30, 40, 50, 60, 70

#### High spread

1, 50, 100, 200, 500

Standard deviation measures this scatter mathematically.
